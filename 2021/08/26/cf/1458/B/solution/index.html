B. Glass Half Spilled

开启传送门

题目描述

There are nnn glasses on the table numbered 1,…,n1, \ldots, n1,…,n. The glass iii can hold up to aia_iai​ units of water, and currently contains bib_ibi​ units of water.

You would like to choose kkk glasses and collect as much water in them as possible. To that effect you can pour water from one glass to another as many times as you like. However, because of the glasses’ awkward shape (and totally unrelated to your natural clumsiness), each time you try to transfer any amount of water, half of the amount is spilled on the floor.

Formally, suppose a glass iii currently contains cic_ici​ units of water, and a glass jjj contains cjc_jcj​ units of water. Suppose you try to transfer xxx units from glass iii to glass jjj (naturally, xxx can not exceed cic_ici​). Then, x/2x / 2x/2 units is spilled on the floor. After the transfer is done, the glass iii will contain ci−xc_i - xci​−x units, and the glass jjj will contain min⁡(aj,cj+x/2)\min(a_j, c_j + x / 2)min(aj​,cj​+x/2) units (excess water that doesn’t fit in the glass is also spilled).

Each time you transfer water, you can arbitrarlly choose from which glass iii to which glass jjj to pour, and also the amount xxx transferred can be any positive real number.

For each k=1,…,nk = 1, \ldots, nk=1,…,n, determine the largest possible total amount of water that can be collected in arbitrarily chosen kkk glasses after transferring water between glasses zero or more times.

Input

The first line contains a single integer nnn (1≤n≤1001 \leq n \leq 1001≤n≤100) — the number of glasses.

The following nnn lines describe the glasses. The iii-th of these lines contains two integers aia_iai​ and bib_ibi​ (0≤bi≤ai≤1000 \leq b_i \leq a_i \leq 1000≤bi​≤ai​≤100, ai>0a_i > 0ai​>0) — capacity, and water amount currently contained for the glass iii, respectively.

Output

Print nnn real numbers — the largest amount of water that can be collected in 1,…,n1,\dots,n1,…,n glasses respectively. Your answer will be accepted if each number is within 10−910^{−9}10−9 absolute or relative tolerance of the precise answer.

Example

Input1

6 5

6 5

10 2

Output2

7.0000000000 11.0000000000 12.0000000000

Note

In the sample case, you can act as follows:

for k=1k=1k=1, transfer water from the first two glasses to the third one, spilling (5+5)/2=5(5+5)/2=5(5+5)/2=5 units and securing 2+(5+5)/2=72+(5+5)/2=72+(5+5)/2=7 units;
for k=2k=2k=2, transfer water from the third glass to any of the first two, spilling 2/2=12/2=12/2=1 unit and securing 5+5+2/2=115+5+2/2=115+5+2/2=11 units;
for k=3k=3k=3, do nothing. All 5+5+2=125+5+2=125+5+2=12 units are secured.

题意

一开始有一些杯子，每个杯子有两个属性 容积 aia_iai​, 已经装水的体积 bib_ibi​。你可以在两个杯子间任意倒水，但是由于杯子长的不太规则，所以每次倒水都会有一半的水倒在地上了。并且任意时刻，杯子中水的数量不能超过容积。

现在需要输出 nnn 个答案，第 iii 个答案意味着，在任意倒水结束后，保留 iii 个水杯中的水，最多能保留多少体积的水。

分析

定义倒水的杯子为源头杯，接水的杯子为目标杯。

首先，我们能轻易的得到，一个杯子不能即作为源头杯又作为目标杯。这个很容易证明，假设我们从杯子 xxx 倒给杯子 yyy，然后又从杯子 yyy 倒给杯子 zzz，那我们为什么不直接让杯子 xxx 倒给杯子 yyy 呢？

所以我们能轻易的得到，答案一定只有一位小数，并且小数位要么是 000，要么是 666。

然后就是一个比较明显的 DP。

我们定义 dp[i][j][k]dp[i][j][k]dp[i][j][k] 意味着，前 iii 个杯子，我们选择其中的 jjj 个杯子，当这 jjj 个杯子容积为 kkk 的时候，拥有的水量。

很显然有转移为 dp[i][j][k]=max(dp[i−1][j−1][k−a]+b)dp[i][j][k] = max(dp[i-1][j-1][k-a] + b)dp[i][j][k]=max(dp[i−1][j−1][k−a]+b)。意味着选最后一个杯子的贡献。

边界条件即为 dp[1…n][j][k]=0dp[1\dots n][j][k] = 0dp[1…n][j][k]=0。

现在有了这个数组，我们怎么统计答案呢？

一个自然的想法是，我们选择这 jjj 个杯子作为目标杯，剩下的 n−jn-jn−j 个杯子作为源头杯。然后一直倒水，直到这 jjj 个杯子倒满为止。基于这个想法我们还需要知道所有杯子的总水量是多少，我们可以在最开始的时候记录这个值，我们设为 BBB。

然后枚举每个 kkk 来统计 jjj 的答案，我们有 ans=max(ans,min(k,dp[n][j][k]+(B−dp[n][j][k])/2))ans = max(ans, min(k, dp[n][j][k] + (B-dp[n][j][k])/2))ans=max(ans,min(k,dp[n][j][k]+(B−dp[n][j][k])/2))。其中 dp[n][j][k]dp[n][j][k]dp[n][j][k] 意味着本来前 jjj 个杯子已经有的水量。那么剩余 n−jn-jn−j 个杯子的水量就是 B−dp[n][j][k]B-dp[n][j][k]B−dp[n][j][k]，其中由于倒水损失一半，所以最多倒 (B−dp[n][j][k])/2(B-dp[n][j][k])/2(B−dp[n][j][k])/2 这么多的水。所以这 jjj 个杯子不考虑容积的情况下，最多可以拥有的水量就是 dp[n][j][k]+(B−dp[n][j][k])/2dp[n][j][k] + (B-dp[n][j][k])/2dp[n][j][k]+(B−dp[n][j][k])/2。然后考虑这 jjj 个杯子的容积为 kkk，所以还需要和 kkk 取个最小值。
